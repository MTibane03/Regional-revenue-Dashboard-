# Regional-revenue-Dashboard-
The dashboard serves an operational purpose, providing the Regional Manager, Nancy Edward, with an operational performance overview of Sonic Selection’s performance in 2013. It focuses on monitoring key metrics such as total revenue (£2,329), customer activity (2,240 customers), and support representative performance over the year. The dashboard is designed to support short-term decision-making by tracking revenue growth and customer trends, particularly in the second half of the year. This enables the manager to evaluate whether the business is achieving its objectives of increasing revenue by 8% and reducing customer churn by 5%.

The intended audience of the dashboard is the Regional Manager and supporting staff responsible for overseeing operations in Canada. As the manager is responsible for driving business performance, the dashboard presents high-level, easy-to-interpret visuals that support quick and informed decisions. Visual elements such as line charts are used to display trends over time, KPI cards highlight key performance indicators, and bar charts and pie charts provide comparative insights into revenue drivers. These design choices ensure that complex data is simplified into actionable insights, allowing the manager to effectively monitor performance and adjust strategies in response to revenue and customer activity trends .
The dashboard consists of several key components designed to effectively communicate business performance. The Revenue KPI card (£2,329) and Customer Count card (2,240) provide immediate high-level insight into overall performance, enabling quick assessment by management. The Revenue per Country bar chart highlights the USA as the leading contributor, allowing the regional manager to identify key markets driving revenue. The two line charts, representing January to June and July to December, illustrate customer trends across the year and demonstrate improved stability in the second half, supporting the objective of reducing customer churn. The Support Representative pie chart shows Jane Peacock handling 36%, Margaret Park 34% and Steve Johnson 30% of customers, enabling evaluation of workload balance and staff performance. Additionally, the pivot table, enhanced with conditional formatting, highlights key anomalies such as the February low (£27.72) and November peak (£49.62), allowing for quick identification of performance fluctuations.
The dashboard applies a consistent blue and white colour scheme, reinforcing professionalism and clarity while ensuring readability. KPI cards are emphasised through bold and larger fonts to draw immediate attention to critical metrics. Conditional formatting uses colour gradients to visually represent performance variations, enabling rapid interpretation without detailed analysis. The inclusion of a reference marker(orange dotted line ) between the two time periods further supports the comparison of performance before and after operational changes. 

Overall, the dashboard indicates mixed performance against the 2013 objectives .While customer churn reduction shows clear improvement through stabilisation in the second half of the year,revenue growth remains inconsistent, as evidenced by the fluctuation between the February low of £27.72 and November peak of £49.62, suggesting the 8% growth target was not consistently achieved and requires targeted intervention in pricing or market diversification strategies. . Furthermore, the heavy reliance on the USA market presents both a key opportunity and a potential risk that should be strategically managed.
